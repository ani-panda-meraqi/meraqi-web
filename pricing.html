Processing tiers set queue position, not render speed: video generation is compute-bound at roughly 3-4 minutes per 45 seconds of finished footage on production hardware. "Seconds" delivery applies to images only.

Daily figures, monthly enforcement: allowances are stated per day for easy comparison, but tracked and enforced monthly. Each plan's monthly pool is the daily figure × 30 (Starter: 6,000 images and 300 videos), and it resets on your personal billing date, the day you signed up or upgraded, not the calendar month. Skip a quiet week and that capacity is still yours to use before the reset. ‡

Annual billing, all paid tiers: "Sign up for the whole year, get two months free." Starter $120, Pro $250, Business $500 per seat per year; exactly two months free on every tier. Monthly price is always the headline price.

Limited-time private beta, ends 09/30/2026: available to the first 300 guests, first come first serve, with a public waitlist beyond 300 (second wave admitted once production capacity is live); unlimited export without watermark, in exchange for patronage and feedback; beta members keep a lifetime Annual Pro rate of $199/year.

† Regulated Industries, what self-hosting means: the platform is deployed inside the customer's own network rather than as shared SaaS: on the customer's own hardware (on-prem) or in their private or government cloud tenancy, reachable from the intranet only, up to fully air-gapped installations with no internet connectivity. All models ship with the deployment and run locally; no third-party APIs are called, and no prompts, content, or telemetry ever leave the customer's environment. Updates arrive as signed offline packages. Quotas are unlimited because generation runs on customer-owned GPUs: capacity is the deployment's hardware, not shared infrastructure. Priced per deployment.

‡ Pacing and fair use: your monthly pool is yours to spend on any schedule, including all at once. To keep delivery fast for everyone, each account runs a limited number of generations at the same time; a very large batch simply queues behind itself and finishes over hours rather than jumping ahead of other customers. Turnaround estimates describe individual jobs as they start, not an entire batch.
