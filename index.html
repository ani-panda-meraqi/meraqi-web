Meraqiμεράκι · meraki

noun · Greek origin

To do something with soul, creativity, and love. To leave a piece of yourself in the work.

We named the platform after the feeling we wanted your work to keep, even when AI handles the heavy lifting. Speed should not cost you your passion and creativity.

The problem

Most AI design tools stop at the first draft.

You type a prompt and get a flat image you cannot really change. Want a different background? Regenerate the whole thing. Want to nudge one button? You cannot. It is baked into pixels.

Most "AI design" tools are image generators with a crop tool bolted on.

Meraqi thinks in layers.

Every design you generate is a structured, multi-layer composition. The headline is a text layer. The background is its own layer. The photo sits on a layer of its own.

You recolor, resize, and rearrange every element the way you would in a real design app, because that is what Meraqi is.

How it works

Four steps from idea to published.

Describe what you want. Meraqi plans it, renders it, and hands you every layer. Then it ships anywhere you need.

01

Describe it

"Instagram post for our spring sale, warm and friendly." Or start from a template and your brand kit.

02

Meraqi plans

Gemma 4 lays out canvas, hierarchy, palette, and type as a DesignSpec. You see the plan before anything renders.

03

Edit every layer

Z-Image renders it to an editable canvas. Refine on the spot, or send a layer to advanced editing for precise masks and compositing.

04

Publish anywhere

Magic Resize reflows to 13+ formats. Schedule straight to Meta, LinkedIn, X, Pinterest, and TikTok.

Video · rolling out

The same soul, in motion.

Meraqi doesn't stop at stills. The engine that keeps a layout on brand also keeps a moving scene consistent from frame to frame, so your spot plays like one continuous shot, not a slideshow.

People, products, camera moves, lighting, and a voice track: up to 45 seconds of continuous 1080p video today, with longer spots rolling out.

No system today generates a long video in one shot; every long video is segments joined together. Meraqi's engine is what holds the segments together: the same people, style, and light from one second to the next.
