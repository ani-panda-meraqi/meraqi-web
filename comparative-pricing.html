What the matrix shows

  • Meraqi is the only vendor pricing in outcomes. Every competitor sells an abstract pool (credits or tokens) whose real-world yield depends on which model you pick; the buyer does the arithmetic. Meraqi states deliverables per day, tracked as a monthly pool (daily figure × 30) from each user's own billing date, so within the month nothing is use-it-or-lose-it.
  • Units are not comparable across vendors. Higgsfield, Artlist, OpenArt and InVideo meter credits per month, each with different model costs per credit; Magnific meters credits per year; Leonardo meters tokens with a rollover bank. A "credits" column that looks bigger is not necessarily more output.
  • Video-first pricing is moving upward. InVideo's current credit plans run $17 to $900 per month, far above the legacy minute-based plans ($25 to $120) still cited by third-party trackers; a price umbrella forming above Meraqi's video tiers.
  • Annual-vs-monthly billing splits obscure headline prices. Higgsfield Ultra is $99 or $129, Artlist Professional is $239.99 or $399.99 depending on the toggle. Meraqi leads with the monthly price everywhere and states annual billing plainly as two months free.
  • Compliance is gated up-tier everywhere. SSO, indemnification, no-training guarantees and SOC 2 live in team or contact-sales tiers across the matrix; the cheapest SSO in the set is Magnific's $55/seat Business (minimum 2 seats), and Higgsfield charges $150/seat for it at Scale. A published Regulated Industries tier (on-prem, gov cloud, air-gapped) has no counterpart anywhere in this set, and Meraqi ships passwordless sign-in with inherited two-step verification on every plan from day one, including single-seat Starter (no passwords stored; Google and Microsoft today, company logins through the Meraqi Identity Broker rolling out), while competitors sell identity features as upsells.
  • Seat economics favor Meraqi. Business at $50/seat undercuts Higgsfield Team ($65-79/seat, no SSO until $150/seat Scale) and Magnific Business ($55-69/seat), matches InVideo's Standard seat ($40-50, which carries only 160 credits/seat), and sits between Leonardo's $24 and $48 team seats.
  • "Unlimited" claims carry fine print. Higgsfield sells 7-day unlimited windows per model; Leonardo's unlimited is relaxed-queue and first-party models only; Artlist reserves unlimited-on-all-models for Custom Business. Meraqi's free month avoids the word entirely: it is simply Starter with a watermark and a relaxed queue, 200 images and 10 videos a day, plainly stated.
